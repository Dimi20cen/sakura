Source files:

This expansion builds on Base CATAN rules unless replaced below.

- Scenario-driven expansion with different setup maps, goals, and victory thresholds.

## Chapter 2: Production-Phase Additions

### Gold fields

- Settlement on producing gold field: take `1` resource of choice.
- City on producing gold field: take `2` resources of choice (any combination).

### Robber vs pirate on 7

- On a `7`, active player may move pirate instead of robber.
- Pirate moves to frame or sea hex and can steal from eligible ship owner on target hex (per rules).

## Chapter 3: Action-Phase Additions

### Ships

- Ships are built on sea edges.
- Ships and roads cannot share the same coastal edge.
- New ship must connect to your existing ship chain or your building (not directly via roads).
- Cannot place ships on edges of pirate hex.

### Ship movement

- May move one ship per turn (subject to restrictions):
  - Ship not built this turn.
  - Ship has an open end.
  - Ship not locked in disallowed closed formations.
  - Pirate restrictions still apply.

### Longest Route with ships

- Longest Route may include roads and ships.
- Road-ship continuity requires connecting through your building.

## Chapter 4: Development Card Adjustments

- Road Building card may build:
  - 2 roads, or
  - 2 ships, or
  - 1 road + 1 ship
- Knight card in Seafarers can activate robber or pirate flow.

## Chapter 5: Scenario Framework

Rulebook campaign includes:

1. Heading for New Shores
2. The Four Islands
3. The Fog Islands
4. Through the Desert
5. The Forgotten Tribe
6. Cloth for Catan
7. The Pirate Islands
8. The Wonders of Catan
9. New World variant

Each scenario defines:

- Unique map/setup
- Special rules
- Specific VP win condition
- Component counts/port/number setup details

For exact per-scenario component matrices, use the rulebook text extract.

## Chapter 6: Scenario-Specific Rule Highlights

### Heading for New Shores

- Initial settlements restricted to main island.
- First settlement on each small island grants bonus VP.
- Scenario-specific win threshold (rulebook value).

### The Four Islands

- Home-island concept per player.
- First settlement on each unexplored island grants bonus VP.
- Scenario-specific win threshold.

### The Fog Islands

- Hidden hex + number discovery system.
- Discovery occurs when route placement reaches eligible edge.
- Land discoveries receive number disc and immediate reward per rules.
- Scenario-specific win threshold.

### Through the Desert

- Large split-island structure with additional settlement/region pressure.
- Scenario-specific placement and scoring rules.
- Scenario-specific win threshold.

### The Forgotten Tribe

- Introduces tribe/village interaction mechanics and rewards.
- Adds scenario-specific exploration/interaction logic.

### Cloth for Catan

- Introduces cloth production/collection economy.
- Cloth contributes to endgame scoring and alternate ending conditions.
- Pirate/robber movement restrictions are scenario-specific.

### The Pirate Islands

- Pirate fleet movement and attack system.
- Warship conversion mechanics.
- Pirate fortress siege/conquest loop.
- Development-card behavior is modified for this scenario.
- Alternate win condition tied to fortress recapture plus VP threshold.

### The Wonders of Catan

- Wonder claiming prerequisites.
- Multi-level wonder building race with high per-level costs.
- Alternate/combined win condition tied to wonder progress and VP.
- Pirate disabled in this scenario.

### New World Variant

- Open-ended island generation and port placement rules.
- Unexplored-island bonus VP rules.
- Scenario-specific default win threshold and setup options.

## Chapter 7: Setup Patterns Across Scenarios

- Starting settlement/road-or-ship constraints vary per scenario.
- Ports may be fixed or randomized depending on scenario.
- Some scenarios include hidden stacks, special tokens, or fortress/village systems.
